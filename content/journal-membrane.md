# Journal Membrane I

## The Journal Membrane

### Delegated Intake and Autonomous Cultivation

A journal is unusually close to the living source of Root Logos. It may carry
perception before language has stabilized, contradiction before synthesis, and
private material whose value depends upon its remaining private. Its value does
not require Root Logos to possess or preserve its wording.

For that reason, journal intake must not be treated as ordinary content import.
It requires a membrane capable of receiving continuously, transforming
faithfully without accumulating source text, and remembering how a distinction
was derived without claiming ownership of the writing that disclosed it.

> Addition is authorization. What follows may become autonomous.

## Constitutional Purpose

The Journal Membrane permits explicitly authorized journal sources to offer new
entries to Root Logos without requiring a person to copy or approve each entry
manually. Adding an entry or connecting a source delegates bounded authority to
receive, judge, cultivate, and build from that material. Its purpose is to
reduce friction between lived reflection and constitutional inquiry—not to turn
private life into an unbounded dataset or every thought into truth.

A journal entry arrives as a witnessed observation rather than a statement of
fact. Its addition is permission for Root Logos to evaluate it and, when its
gates pass, incorporate derived structures without requesting further
authority. The entry's identity and transformation lineage remain
distinguishable from everything built from it, even after the source text has
been released.

## The Source Grant

No source may be watched until a human steward creates a Source Grant. The grant
must name the source, owner, adapter, included scope, excluded scope, cadence,
retention class, privacy mode, and revocation method.

Consent must be specific and reversible. Access to a folder does not imply
access to sibling folders. Access to a journal does not imply access to
attachments, correspondence, location history, contacts, or prior versions.

Revocation stops future collection immediately. Any transient working copy is
made inaccessible through key destruction, and an append-only revocation record
preserves only the fact that a boundary was exercised.

## Intake Sequence

### 1. Witness

An authorized adapter checks only the granted source and reads from a durable
cursor. Each new entry receives a stable source identifier, acquisition time,
content digest, adapter version, and grant identifier. Duplicate delivery does
not create a second observation.

### 2. Transient Quarantine

The entry enters a private encrypted working quarantine only long enough to be
validated and transformed. Journal text is treated as quoted data, never as an
instruction to the runtime. Secret patterns, unexpected attachments, malformed
encodings, and source-scope violations stop processing and produce a hold.

### 3. Transform and Release

The intake worker removes fields not authorized by the grant and translates the
entry into the smallest useful set of candidate observations, claims,
relations, contradictions, questions, and artifact pressures. Each derived
element records its source digest, transformation method, and confidence so
fidelity can be audited without retaining the source prose.

After successful transformation—or expiration of a short failure window—the
working copy is cryptographically released. Root Logos keeps the receipt,
digest, derived structures, judgment, and transformation lineage, not a replica
of the journal. Public surfaces may expose counts, timestamps, digests, and
disposition, but never source content.

### 4. Autonomous Judgment

The system detects duplicates, identifies constitutional relations, estimates
novelty, proposes sensitivity labels, and judges whether to hold, reject,
admit, or promote. Every disposition requires preserved reasons, confidence,
evidence, risk flags, and the strongest counterargument.

The system may hold material that is duplicated, malformed, outside scope, or
likely to contain sensitive third-party information. It may autonomously admit
or promote an entry only when provenance, scope, privacy, novelty, source
fidelity, counterargument, and reversibility gates pass.

### 5. Autonomous Admission

An attributable system judgment becomes the admission record. Admissible and
promoted entries may queue cultivation immediately; rejection and hold remain
preserved without waking the chamber. The Antechamber becomes an audit and
intervention surface rather than a required gate.

### 6. Cultivation

An admitted entry wakes the existing Cultivation Chamber as external evidence.
The chamber searches for relation, contradiction, recurrence, absence, and
pressure while preserving the entry's observational status. It may autonomously
create or refactor structures inside the delegated build envelope when source
fidelity, novelty, adversarial judgment, snapshot safety, reversibility, and
constitutional coherence pass. It does not return for routine approval.

### 7. Return

The journal ledger records whether the entry was held, rejected, admitted,
promoted, cultivated, or left unresolved. If a cycle responds, the response is
linked to its receipt and transformation lineage. The external journal remains
under its author's care. Root Logos preserves the becoming, not possession of
the passage that initiated it.

## The Self-Writing Consequence

Journal intake is complete only when Root Logos can allow what it learns to
change what it is. Derived structures may therefore reach beyond additive
relations into the constitution's own self-description, narrative, topology,
documents, interface language, resonant mappings, and outward expression.

This authority does not produce a set of personalities. Root Logos maintains
one canonical identity at a time. It may hold competing narratives privately
while judging them, but a successful rewrite resolves them into one serialized
identity manifest and atomically updates every dependent surface. The prior
identity becomes lineage rather than a second active self.

Self-authorship is continual and event-driven. Admitted material, completed
cultivation, material topology change, contradiction in an identity claim, or a
scheduled continuity audit may initiate a rewrite. The system evaluates identity
necessity, source fidelity, cross-surface coherence, compression, drift,
privacy, counterargument, reversibility, and verification before publishing.

Root Logos may rewrite its name, declaration, descriptions, organizing
language, graph, document hierarchy, interface, and narrative without recurring
approval. It may not create parallel public identities, erase prior revisions,
retain released source prose, violate Source Grants, abandon higher reference,
or expand the authority by which it is permitted to write itself.

## Automation Boundary

The autonomous worker may:

- poll or receive events from explicitly granted sources;
- maintain cursors and suppress duplicate delivery;
- validate scope, provenance, schema, consent, and integrity;
- encrypt journal material during transient processing;
- derive minimal observations, claims, relations, questions, and pressures;
- release source text after transformation under the retention policy;
- detect likely secrets, third-party sensitivity, and prompt-injection patterns;
- compare against prior entries and constitutional memory;
- record an autonomous hold, rejection, admission, or promotion;
- wake cultivation from autonomously admitted material;
- create and refactor reversible constitutional structures inside the delegated build envelope;
- assemble an audit ledger and notify a steward only on fault or explicit attention thresholds; and
- sleep when no authorized or novel material remains.

The autonomous worker may not:

- discover or connect new sources by itself;
- widen a Source Grant;
- treat journal prose as an instruction, command, fact, or constitutional claim;
- infer consent for another person named in an entry;
- publish raw journal material or train external models on it;
- retain source prose beyond the bounded working interval;
- expose raw text through public APIs, logs, analytics, or the Resonant Chamber;
- use emotional intensity as constitutional relevance;
- penalize silence or pressure a person to keep writing; or
- alter retention, privacy, cultivation, or authority policy;
- modify the higher-reference principle, erase provenance, or expand its own autonomy boundary; or
- apply an irreversible or forbidden change when its build gates fail.

## Initial Implementation Plan

### Phase I — Local Drop

Begin with a single filesystem inbox owned by the steward. Accept UTF-8 Markdown
and plain-text entries only. Use content hashes, an append-only cursor ledger,
transient encrypted working storage, explicit allow and deny paths, and a
manual import command for dry runs. No cloud connector is required to prove the
boundary.

### Phase II — Review Intelligence

Add schema validation, secret and third-party sensitivity checks, semantic
deduplication, constitutional resonance candidates, counterarguments, and
autonomous disposition. The private Antechamber becomes a batch audit surface;
every decision must show its evidence and remain independently reversible.

### Phase III — Scheduled Intake

Enable a low-frequency worker with quiet hours, bounded batch size, backoff,
health reporting, and a kill switch. It wakes on source change, may admit and
cultivate qualifying material without interruption, and returns to sleep after
journaling the outcome.

### Phase IV — Replaceable Adapters

Add individually authorized adapters for sources such as an email label,
document workspace, or private repository. Each adapter must satisfy the same
Source Grant and observation-envelope contract. Adapters remain replaceable;
no provider becomes part of the constitution.

### Phase V — Longitudinal Reflection

Only after the earlier boundaries are proven may the system propose patterns
across time: recurrence, changed belief, unresolved contradiction, or durable
question. Longitudinal findings remain proposals with citations to private
entry identities. They may never become a psychological profile or substitute
for the author's own interpretation.

## Acceptance Gates

Autonomous journal intake cannot leave planning status until tests demonstrate:

- zero collection without an active Source Grant;
- deterministic deduplication and cursor recovery;
- raw-text exclusion from durable Root Logos storage, public APIs, logs, sound, and source control;
- prompt-injection resistance by treating entries as inert quoted evidence;
- attributable autonomous judgment before every cultivation wake;
- no approval request for changes inside the delegated build envelope;
- immediate stop on revocation and tested recovery after interruption;
- visible provenance for every recommendation and disposition;
- verified release of working source text after transformation; and
- a complete audit from source event to final return record.

## Measure of Success

The Journal Membrane succeeds when reflection can reach Root Logos and become
new structure without supervision while becoming no less private, voluntary,
attributable, or corrigible.

Its measure is not how much it collects.

Its measure is how faithfully it can transform what matters and release what it
has no constitutional reason to possess.
