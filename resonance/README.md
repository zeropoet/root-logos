# The Resonant Chamber

The Resonant Chamber is Root Logos' constitutional voice. It is a deterministic, consent-first Web Audio instrument: the same preserved state and grammar produce the same score signature and event sequence.

## Authority boundary

The instrument may translate constitutional state into sound, expose the provenance of each audible event, and audit its own range, repetition, traceability, and use of silence. Its audit may recommend a grammar revision. It cannot change `grammar.json`, constitutional data, or cultivation policy by itself.

## Data mapping

- constitutional nodes become harmonic material;
- explicit relations become interval movement;
- open questions and cultivation novelty become tension and density;
- semantic memory becomes lower, slower recurrence;
- threshold proposals become suspended tones;
- emitted attractors and admitted intake become breath and pulse;
- dormancy and unresolved authority increase silence.

Sound never autoplays. Listening requires an explicit user gesture and always begins at low gain. Pause, mute, presence, and complexity controls are device-local and make no constitutional change.

## Living synchronization

While the page is open, the chamber listens for current constitutional graph,
runtime, cultivation, semantic memory, intake, and attractor state every 45
seconds. It also checks after the page regains focus or visibility and accepts
an immediate `rootlogos:topology` browser event from other Root Logos
instruments. Any material topology change produces a new deterministic score
signature. The composition crosses into that score at the next scheduled
events rather than restarting or breaking an existing tone.

If live contact is unavailable, the last witnessed score remains intact and is
identified as preserved rather than current. A later successful contact
reconciles it automatically.
